Q2: How does a student begin the process of requesting disability accommodations at JHU?

Expected answer:
  Complete the online SDS Application and provide documentation meeting SDS Guidelines for Documentation

System response:
To begin the process of requesting disability accommodations at JHU, a student should complete the online SDS Application through the university-wide database, Accommodation Information Manager (AIM), and then submit documentation. The student will then attend an Initial Meeting with an SDS Advisor to discuss their needs and potential accommodations and services.

Q4: What is the Dissertation Prize Fellowship and who is eligible for it?

Expected answer:
  A fellowship for final-year KSAS students to focus on dissertation writing without teaching obligations for a semester; students beyond their sixth year in spring 2025 are ineligible

System response:
The Dissertation Prize Fellowship is a program offered by the Office of the Dean of Arts and Sciences, designed to allow final-year students to focus on dissertation writing without any teaching obligation for a semester. To be eligible, applicants must be graduate students in the Krieger School of Arts and Sciences and complete their dissertation by the end of the award period. Priority is given to students in their sixth year, and students beyond their sixth year in spring 2025 are ineligible.

Q5: What happens to a School of Education student who fails to enroll each semester and does not receive an approved leave of absence?

Expected answer:
  They are made inactive and dropped from the rolls in the second week after the start of the semester, and must reapply for admission

System response:
A School of Education student who fails to enroll each semester and does not receive an approved leave of absence will be made inactive and dropped from the rolls due to enrollment inactivity in the second week after the start of the semester. The student will need to reapply for admission.
